Project Overview

The Olive Health App was a project where I developed a prototype for a tool designed to help users manage their health documents. The objective was to create an easy-to-use platform where users could upload, organize, and access important medical records. My role involved conducting thorough user research, creating wireframes, and developing a functional prototype using Figma.

Role:UX Researcher & Designer Objective:To design a user-friendly health document management prototype Duration:10 months

Project Research

I began by conducting interviews and surveys with potential users to understand their struggles with managing health documents. Through this research, I identified key pain points such as difficulty organizing and accessing medical records quickly when needed.

Methods used:User interviews, surveys, personas, and mental models. Outcome:These insights helped guide the creation of user personas and user flows.

Project Wireframes

After defining user needs, I created low-fidelity wireframes to map out basic functionalities. These wireframes evolved into mid- and high-fidelity versions, where I focused on the app’s visual hierarchy and ease of use. Throughout the process, I incorporated user feedback to refine the layout.

Tools:Hand-drawn sketches for initial designs, then Figma for high-fidelity wireframes.

Project Prototyping

The final stage was creating a clickable, high-fidelity prototype in Figma. This prototype included key features like document upload, categorization, and secure sharing. It was tested with users to ensure the flow was intuitive and easy to navigate.

Project Usability Testing

I conducted usability tests, analyzing feedback to identify areas of improvement. The tests highlighted the importance of simplifying the document upload process and improving the visibility of key actions.

Methods:Usability testing (planning, scripting, conducting, analyzing results), preference testing.

Conclution

This project taught me the importance of aligning user needs with business goals, especially in a critical field like health management. Through iterations and usability testing, the prototype evolved into a more user-centric tool. The final prototype simplifies the document management process, helping users save time and avoid frustration.
